# Reading Path: For Students

Your generation is the first to enter the workforce alongside AI --- not as a novelty, but as a baseline. This path helps you understand what skills to build when the machine already handles the average.

The book's first reader was a high school senior on the humanities track who hates probability and skipped every math problem. She still understood entropy by the end. If she could get through it, so can you.

---

## Stop 1: The Affluent Society (丰饶社会) — Why 80 Is the New Zero

Start here. This chapter opens with a question you will face in every job interview for the rest of your career: when AI can write a love letter ten times better than you, is that augmentation or forgery? The core idea is **intelligence inflation** --- when AI lifts everyone to 80 out of 100, 80 becomes the new zero. Standard answers are now commodity output. This matters to you because the degree you are chasing was designed for a world where producing competent work was hard. That world is ending.

Read more: [Intelligence Inflation](/wiki/concepts/intelligence-inflation) | [Augmentation vs. Forgery](/wiki/concepts/augmentation-vs-forgery)

## Stop 2: The Sinew Transformation Classic (易筋经) — What to Actually Train

Now that you know what is losing value, this chapter maps what gains it. The key concept is **verification asymmetry**: tasks that are easy to verify (translate this paragraph, summarize this article) get automated first. Tasks that are hard to verify (is this strategy sound? does this design have taste?) become the new scarcity. Train the hard-to-verify skills --- judgment, taste, ethics, strategic thinking. Your major matters less than your ability to develop these.

Read more: [Verification Asymmetry](/wiki/concepts/verification-asymmetry) | [Sinew Classic as Skill Retraining](/wiki/bridges/sinew-classic-is-skill-retraining)

## Stop 3: Star-Absorbing Skill (吸星大法) — How to Use AI Without Losing Yourself

This chapter tells the story of Xiao Lin, an accountant whose salary rises because of AI, not despite it --- because she learned to wield it as a lever for judgment. The Diamond Sutra opens the chapter: all of AI's outputs are conditioned phenomena. What is unconditioned is your judgment, your values, your ability to decide what matters. This is the practical chapter --- how to actually collaborate with AI as a tool rather than surrender to it as a replacement.

Read more: [Star-Absorbing Skill as Transfer Learning](/wiki/bridges/star-absorbing-is-transfer-learning)

## Stop 4: The Letter Exchange — A Father Writes to His Daughter

Read the daughter's unedited feedback and the father's reply. She is your peer --- skeptical, funny, impatient with jargon. His reply ends with the book's single most important line: "AI can do everything within the distribution. What you can give this world lies outside the distribution." This is not motivational fluff. It is a precise technical claim about where human value lives.

Read the full exchange: [Letter (English)](/letter) | [Letter (Chinese)](/letter/zh)

## Stop 5: Intelligence Ladder (智能阶梯) — From Parrot to Scientist

Now zoom out. Where is AI actually strong, and where does it still fail? This chapter maps the spectrum from mimicry to genuine scientific reasoning. Current LLMs excel at in-distribution tasks but struggle with the rare and unusual --- exactly the frontier where human uniqueness lives. Understanding this spectrum helps you calibrate your anxiety: AI is not coming for everything, but it is coming for the average.

Read more: [Moonlight Box as Autoregression](/wiki/bridges/moonlight-box-is-autoregression)
