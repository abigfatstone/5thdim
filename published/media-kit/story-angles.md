# Story Angles for Media

Five ready-to-pitch angles for covering *The Fifth Dimension* by Xun Jiang.

---

## 1. A Father's Letter on the Eve of the Storm

**Headline suggestion:** "He Wrote an AI Survival Guide for His Daughter -- Three Months Before Her College Exam"

An AI practitioner in China writes a book for his humanities-track daughter as she prepares for the gaokao, the most consequential exam in any Chinese student's life. She hates probability, skips the math problems, yet tells him she finally understands entropy. The book becomes a family letter about what to hold onto when the rules are being rewritten.

**Best fit:** Parenting and education outlets, lifestyle features, Sunday magazine profiles
**Interview topics:** Writing for a non-technical audience, the gaokao as a cultural pressure point, how a father talks to his child about an uncertain future

---

## 2. Zhuangzi Meets GPT: Eastern Philosophy as AI Framework

**Headline suggestion:** "What a 2,300-Year-Old Chinese Philosopher Can Teach Us About Artificial Intelligence"

Each chapter is named after a spell from wuxia martial arts fiction. Loss functions are explained through Zhuangzi's "useless tree." Human-AI collaboration opens with the Diamond Sutra. The Bitter Lesson of AI research is framed as a rediscovery of Daoist *liubai* -- the blank space in a painting. This is not cultural seasoning; it is the structural architecture of the argument.

**Best fit:** Culture and ideas sections, philosophy and tech crossover publications, Asian diaspora media
**Interview topics:** How Eastern and Western frameworks illuminate different aspects of AI, wuxia as a teaching tool, why simplicity beats complexity across civilizations

---

## 3. Inside View: What a Chinese AI Practitioner Sees That Silicon Valley Doesn't

**Headline suggestion:** "From Shanda to the Fifth Dimension: An Insider's Map of China's AI Landscape"

Xun Jiang works with Chen Tianqiao, who built one of China's largest gaming companies and then pivoted to brain science and AI. The book emerges from that vantage point -- where deep data matters more than big data, where the gaokao shapes how an entire generation thinks about competition, and where a billion people are encountering AI abundance for the first time.

**Best fit:** Tech and business publications, foreign affairs and China-focused media, industry newsletters
**Interview topics:** Deep data vs. big data, how Chinese students and parents are navigating AI anxiety, what "co-creating with AI" looks like in practice

---

## 4. "80 Is the New Zero" -- When AI Rewrites the Meaning of Scarcity

**Headline suggestion:** "When Everyone Scores 80, What Does It Mean to Be Exceptional?"

The book's central thesis reframes the AI debate: the threat is not that machines will take our jobs, but that they will make average performance worthless. When AI lifts all abilities to 80 out of 100, the definition of scarcity shifts from production to authenticity, perspective, and connection. The love-letter problem makes it vivid: when AI writes better than you, the value is not in the words but in the willingness to write them yourself.

**Best fit:** Economics and future-of-work outlets, opinion pages, podcasts on technology and society
**Interview topics:** Verification asymmetry (easy-to-verify tasks get automated), the love-letter problem, what "outside the distribution" means for career choices

---

## 5. The Wuxia Way: Martial Arts Philosophy as an AI Survival Framework

**Headline suggestion:** "Star-Absorbing Skill and the Sinew Transformation Classic: A Martial Arts Guide to the AI Age"

The ten chapters are named after mythological and wuxia "spells" -- from the Teleportation Array (connectivity) to the Sinew Transformation Classic (mastering judgment). The martial arts metaphor is not decorative: it captures how individuals must train internal capabilities that cannot be absorbed from outside. "A ruler is not for measuring the dragon -- it is for summoning it."

**Best fit:** Creative and cultural criticism, gaming and pop culture outlets, book review sections
**Interview topics:** How wuxia fiction maps onto technical concepts, the Chinese literary tradition of explaining the world through myth, why metaphor is essential for AI literacy
